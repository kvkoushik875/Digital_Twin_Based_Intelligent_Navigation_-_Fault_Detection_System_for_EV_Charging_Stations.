# About the Project
The growing adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) has spurred the expansion of the electric charging infrastructure in day-to-day life. Electric Vehicle charging stations have also experienced some faults, that includes voltage fluctuations, power loss, equipment and communication failures, and overheating. Therefore, it is essential to detect these kinds of faults in real time and divert the Electric Vehicle users to alternate charging stations. This study  proposes a digital twin-based intelligent navigation and fault detection system for electric vehicle charging stations. A Digital Twin technology is used to simulate a physical electric vehicle charging station, allowing users to monitor and simulate charging parameters in real time. Therefore, by comparing actual and predicted parameters, deviations such as voltage and power losses. In addition, deep learning–based anomaly detection learns normal operational patterns and detects abnormal states such as voltage instability, excessive power loss, charger overheating, communication system failures, and interruptions to charging sessions. Once a problem is detected, the system will assess the charging station's health state and notify users via a navigation interface. The proposed method improves the reliability, efficiency, and management of charging infrastructure for EVs, which will lead to the development of intelligent and sustainable smart mobility systems
